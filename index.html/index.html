Wheat Yellow Rust
Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici
High severity Wheat Fungal
AI Confidence Score 94%
Chemical Treatment
Apply Propiconazole 25% EC at 0.1% concentration. Spray 500–750 ml/ha. Repeat after 15 days if infection persists.
Organic Option
Spray neem oil (5ml/L) + garlic extract. Use sulfur dust (20 kg/ha) as a preventive measure. Apply early morning.
Application Steps
  • Remove and destroy heavily infected leaves immediately to reduce spore load
  • Mix fungicide at recommended concentration in clean water
  • Apply in the early morning or evening to avoid leaf burn
  • Ensure thorough coverage of all leaf surfaces, especially undersides
  • Monitor for 5–7 days. If no improvement, consult a local agronomist
Prevention Strategy
  • Plant resistant varieties: HD-3086, PBW-343, or other locally recommended rust-resistant cultivars
  • Avoid dense sowing — maintain proper row spacing to improve air circulation
  • Apply balanced fertiliser — avoid excess nitrogen which promotes lush growth susceptible to rust
  • Monitor crops weekly during cool, humid weather (Nov–Feb in North India)
  • Apply prophylactic fungicide spray at tillering stage in high-risk zones
About Yellow Rust
Yellow rust (stripe rust) is one of the most damaging wheat diseases worldwide, caused by the obligate fungal pathogen Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici. It thrives in cool, moist conditions (10–15°C) and spreads via wind-borne spores. Yellow-orange stripes form along leaf veins, severely reducing photosynthesis. In severe outbreaks, yield losses can reach 70%. The disease spreads rapidly in dense crops and can devastate entire fields within 2–3 weeks if untreated.
